## New Genre Specs

### Genre 7: Investigation & Journalism
- Party as HoloNet reporters, Senate investigators, independent truth-seekers
- Setting hooks: OFA's detention of 216 Tier 2+ individuals, Czerka Telos fraud, Triumvirate's existence (undiscovered by authorities), Jendri reparations petition
- Fate mechanics: Investigate + Contacts + Notice as primary skills; information as treasure; social conflicts with sources
- "What Can Go Wrong" section on investigation stalling

### Genre 8: Trade & Economics
- Party runs commercial operations; kolto, beskar, cortisis, technology, refugees
- Setting hooks: Manaan's kolto monopoly, beskar forge access, Czerka supply chains, Exchange waypoints, Outer Rim resource scarcity
- Fate mechanics: Resources as stress track (supply/demand pressure), trade negotiations as contests, cargo as aspects
- Strongest at Regional scope

### Genre 9: Religious Schism
- Party navigates competing theological positions on the Force
- Setting hooks: Jedi's 5 factions, Sith Keepers vs. Triumvirate philosophy, Mandalorian Creed fracture (integration vs. tradition), Force crisis as theological challenge
- Fate mechanics: Belief aspects compelled constantly, social conflicts as debates, faction clocks for theological movements
- Campaign question: what does the Force owe its users? What do its users owe it?

### Genre 10: Reconstruction
- Party builds rather than destroys; post-conflict communities
- Setting hooks: Telos restoration, Cathar resettlement, Onderon after Second Siege, Serroco mining communities, frontier settlements
- Fate mechanics: Community as character (Fate Fractal), challenge frameworks for infrastructure, resource management
- Strongest at Personal/Regional scope

### Combining Genres
- Most campaigns blend 2-3 genres across acts
- Table mapping genres to scope tiers
- Example combinations with Act I/II/III genre shifts

## Part III: Campaign Scope and Structure

### Scope Tiers
**Personal (1 world, 1 community):** Named NPCs, local politics, specific problems. Best for mini-campaigns. Genres: Frontier, Reconstruction, Trade.
**Regional (3-7 systems, 1-2 factions):** Faction operations, supply lines, territorial disputes. Standard campaigns. Genres: Military, Espionage, Political.
**Galactic (multiple regions, 3+ factions):** Force crisis, Triumvirate conspiracy, succession politics. Long campaigns. Genres: Space Opera, Force Mystery, Political.

### Episode Structures
**Mini-campaign (3-6 episodes):** Single arc. Definitive ending. One scope tier. One primary genre. Best for actual play. Template: Episode 1 (situation + stakes), Episodes 2-4 (complications + escalation), Episode 5-6 (climax + resolution).

**Standard campaign (7-12 episodes):** Two arcs or one arc with a mid-campaign pivot. One scope tier expanding to two. Primary + secondary genre. Template: Episodes 1-3 (Act I: discovery), Episodes 4-9 (Act II: action), Episodes 10-12 (Act III: decision).

**Long campaign (13-20 episodes):** Three arcs. All three scope tiers. Genre blending. Template: Episodes 1-5 (Personal scope), Episodes 6-13 (Regional scope), Episodes 14-20 (Galactic scope).

### Actual Play Guidance
- Prefer mini-campaigns with definitive endings
- Each mini-campaign is a complete story
- Season structure: 3-4 mini-campaigns can share continuity (same galaxy, different parties or same party, different problems)
- Episode target: 60-90 minutes of edited audio per episode
- Session target: 2.5-3.5 hours of recorded play per episode of edited content

### Campaign Planning Worksheet
- Scope tier selection
- Primary + secondary genre
- Episode count target
- 3 faction clocks
- 5 key NPCs (2 wants each)
- 3 locations with defined tensions
- Central question (one sentence)
- Definitive ending condition

## Part IV: Fate at the Table

### Aspects as Campaign Architecture
- High concepts encode faction loyalty
- Troubles encode setting tensions
- Relationship aspects create party cohesion
- Situation aspects from the setting book (dead zones, faction presence, economic conditions)
- Compels drive 60%+ of narrative momentum

### Skill List in the Concordat Era
Map all 19 Fate skills to setting-specific applications:
- Fight/Shoot: combat in dead zones (no Force support), Mandalorian arena combat
- Investigate: OFA surveillance, Triumvirate trail, archaeological research
- Contacts: faction networks, Exchange information markets, diaspora communities
- Resources: kolto costs, ship maintenance, beskar trade
- Lore: Force traditions, Sith history, Rakatan archaeology
- Will: Force Instability resistance, dead zone endurance
- etc.

### Force Instability Mechanics
- Standing compel: GM can compel any Force-related aspect once per session without spending a fate point
- Dead zone scaling: Partial (invoke difficulty), Significant (compel without FP), Total (automatic failure, FP to attempt)
- Force technique degradation: specific techniques fail in specific orders
- Miraluka special case: Force Empathy baseline, dead zones = blindness

### Dead Zone Encounters
- Treat as hostile environment with situation aspects
- Zone entry: Will overcome (Fair +2 in Partial, Great +4 in Significant)
- Ongoing pressure: stress each scene from environmental aspects
- Equipment: lightsabers function (plasma), Force-enhanced items lose enhancement
- Navigation: hyperspace near dead zones requires Pilot overcome at increased difficulty

### Contests, Challenges, Conflicts
- **Contest:** Kolto trade negotiation (Resources vs. Resources), hyperspace race, intelligence extraction
- **Challenge:** Telos reconstruction milestone, dead zone traversal, diplomatic summit
- **Conflict:** Fleet engagement (zone-based), Senate debate (social), Jedi Council hearing

### Zone-Based Conflict
- Ship combat: zones = range bands (Close, Short, Medium, Long), each zone has situation aspects (asteroid field, dead zone proximity, debris)
- Political arena: zones = audiences (Senate floor, committee room, media gallery, private office), movement between zones changes who hears you
- Dead zone: zones = severity gradient (edge, partial, significant, total), deeper = more danger + more reward

### Fate Fractal: Factions as Characters
- Each faction has aspects, skills, stress tracks, consequences
- Faction actions happen on faction clocks between sessions
- Party can inflict consequences on factions (expose OFA → faction takes "Public Scrutiny" consequence)
- Factions can inflict consequences on parties (Concordat intelligence → party takes "Wanted" situation aspect)
