# Campaign Pitch Redesign — Design Spec
**Date:** 2026-04-01

## Problem
The three Epic pitches (17-19) converge on the Star Forge, read like novel outlines, and lack session-level gameplay guidance. The user wants campaigns optimized for actual play tables.

## Decisions

### Scope
- Keep Pitch 18 (The Long War) core, overhaul to remove Star Forge from Arcs 4-5
- Replace Pitch 17 (Concordat Ascendant) with The Hunted
- Replace Pitch 19 (Dying of the Light) with The Horror
- Add The Frontier as a new Long campaign
- Add The Heist as a new Standard campaign
- Total: 21 pitches (up from 19)

### Renumbering
Old Pitches 13-16 become 14-17. Old Pitch 18 becomes 19 (content overhauled). Pitches 1-12 unchanged.

### Design Decisions
1. **The Frontier** — Long tier (13-20 sessions), 3-4 arcs, community-building at a frontier station
2. **The Heist handler** — Independent antiquities broker who works across factions, deals in high-end artifacts
3. **The Horror degradation** — Narrative only (GM compels, descriptive consequences), no mechanical technique removal
4. **The Hunted inciting incident** — Four options presented in the pitch, keyed to party composition:
   - A: Triumvirate severance test (Force-sensitive parties)
   - B: OFA black site (Concordat-adjacent parties)
   - C: Nihilus feeding event (dead zone proximity parties)
   - D: Concordat war crime cover-up (military veteran parties)

### The Heist (Standard, Pitch 13, ~10 sessions)
Heist + Trade and Economics. A crew of specialists pulls five escalating jobs for an independent antiquities broker. Jobs progress from training heist (Czerka warehouse) through social infiltration (Hutt viewing party), moral line (Jedi Enclave theft), job-goes-wrong (Manaan underwater extraction), to the big score (Korriban tomb). The fifth job's buyer is the Triumvirate. Three named NPCs: the broker, a competing crew leader, a Concordat customs inspector.

### The Frontier (Long, Pitch 18, ~16 sessions)
Frontier Survival + Reconstruction + Trade. The party settles at Beacon Station, a struggling Outer Rim waypoint. Sixteen sessions of community building: supply crises, raider defense, dead zone investigation, trade fair, political reckoning. The station is a Fate character with aspects and stress. Four named NPCs: station administrator, repair tech, Selkath physician, Remnant liaison.

### The Long War Overhaul (Epic, Pitch 19)
Keep Arcs 1-3 (supply circuit, intelligence war, breaking point). Replace Arc 4 (remove Star Forge/Shadowlands/Unknown Regions) with The Fracture: Revan's permanent collapse, fleet splintering, loyalty tour, defection crisis. Replace Arc 5 with redesigned War's End: armistice or offensive without Star Forge as a factor. Kaeranth's decision becomes pure political/moral climax about the Remnant's purpose independent of Revan.

### The Hunted (Epic, Pitch 20, 50-60 sessions)
Espionage + Frontier Survival + Political Intrigue. Five arcs: The Witness (inciting incident, initial flight), The Run (galaxy-crossing evasion), The Network (building underground support), The Offensive (using evidence as leverage), The Reckoning (forcing confrontation). Three named NPCs: pursuit operative, fellow witness, sympathetic insider.

### The Horror (Epic, Pitch 21, 55-65 sessions)
Horror + Investigation + Military Operations. Five arcs: The First Silence (encounter Nihilus aftermath), The Hunt (tracking feeding patterns), The Gathering (assembling strike team), The Approach (closing in, Force degradation intensifies), The Silence (final confrontation). Three named NPCs: Visas Marr (survivor), Colonel Tobin (Concordat investigator), Sion (guardian). Nihilus is not a character; Nihilus is an event.
