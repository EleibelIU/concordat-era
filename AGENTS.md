# The Concordat Era — Project Instructions

## Project Overview

This is a Star Wars KOTOR-era alternate history TTRPG setting book for Fate Condensed, built for an actual play podcast. The primary document is `Homebrewery/Setting Book.md`. This repository is the source of truth; Homebrewery drifts and should be used for rendering only.

The setting reimagines the post-Mandalorian Wars galaxy through Napoleonic historical parallels: Malak governs as Supreme Chancellor-General (First Consul) with permanent emergency powers, a surveillance apparatus targeting Force-sensitives, and military governors replacing elected officials. Revan returned from the Unknown Regions with a fracturing mind and runs a government-in-exile through Admiral Kaeranth. The Force is dying, and no one agrees on why.

**Campaign Year:** 4 CE (Concordat Era). Three years after the Concordat's founding.

---

## Setting Thesis and Tone

**Thesis:** The Concordat Era is about what happens when the foundations everyone built their lives on turn out to be temporary. Three governments claim legitimacy; none deserve it without qualification. Players enter a galaxy where the old answers no longer work and no one has agreed on the new ones yet.

**Tone:** Political thriller crossed with frontier survival story. Post-revolutionary, not post-apocalyptic. The Directory period after the French Revolution: exhausted, paranoid pragmatism.

**Player Promise:** You will play characters who matter in a galaxy that has not decided what it is yet. You will find people with defensible positions doing indefensible things, and you will have to decide where you stand.

## The Force Crisis

The Force is failing. Sith mass ritual warfare during the Great Hyperspace War started the damage. The Ashen Campaign's forty-one strikes deepened it. Dead zones are expanding.

**Dead Zone Scale:** Partial (intermittent, ~50%), Significant (resists use, precognition fragments), Total (Force absent, Miraluka go blind, lightsabers still cut as plasma weapons).

**Competing Theories:** Jedi scholars think using the Force accelerates damage. Sith scholars think ten thousand years of treating the Force as a tool broke the relationship. Revan believes the Force is an ecosystem and dead zones are an immune response. Jolee Bindo observes the Kashyyyk Shadowlands ecology is stable and untouched.

**Material impact:** Hyperspace unreliable. Force healing fails without warning. Kolto is the galaxy's most valuable resource.

## Napoleonic Parallels

These parallels inform every major faction, character, and institution.

**Government:** Three-consul system (Malak/Mandalore/Senate Speaker) mirrors Napoleon's Consulate. Emergency powers with no sunset clause. Military governors replacing local autonomy. Office of Force Affairs parallels Fouché's police state. Hybrid civil-military administration blends Republic systems with Mandalorian hierarchy.

**Military:** The Ashen Campaign parallels Napoleonic artillery doctrine (concentration of force at decisive points). Professional standing army replaced the Republic's peacetime patrol navy. Kaeranth's career tracks French officers who trained under the ancien régime and fought under Napoleon.

**Characters:** Malak as Napoleon (pragmatic authoritarian, martial proof, physical decline, administrative obsession, democratic theater over absolute authority). Revan as the exile-genius (Napoleon at St. Helena, chosen exile, fracturing mind, predictions that come true). Kaeranth as Berthier (indispensable chief of staff translating brilliance into actionable orders). Concordat internal factions mirror post-revolutionary French politics: Militarists, Consolidationists, Restorationists, Expansionists.

**Tonal Period:** Directory/Congress of Vienna. Governments stabilizing after total warfare. Exhausted institutional pragmatism, not revolutionary fervor.

## Literary Parallels and Influences

**Historical writing:** Character sections follow Napoleonic biography structure (Andrew Roberts, Adam Zamoyski). Military history scholarship: supply chain collapse, administrative failure, attrition calculus receive the same weight as battles.

**Literary fiction:** The Silmarillion (prose rhythm, cosmological weight, encyclopedic lore). King Lear (Revan's mental fracture, prophecy and incoherence in alternation). Classical tragedy (no pure villains, defensible positions doing indefensible things). KOTOR II philosophy (Kreia's arguments about Force-as-enslavement taken seriously as philosophy).

**Genre structure:** Political thriller (factions' internal contradictions, intelligence networks, competing agendas). Frontier survival (Outer Rim self-reliance, kolto costs, hyperspace failures). Theological debate as plot (the Force crisis framed philosophically and materially).

## Skills

Two skills govern work in this repo. Both are mandatory when their conditions apply.

### Brainstorming (Design Before Implementation)

**Trigger:** Any creative work. New sections, new characters, new worlds, new species entries, structural changes, feature additions. If you are about to write new content or restructure existing content, brainstorm first.

**Hard gate:** Do not write prose, create sections, or modify structure until the design has been presented and approved. This applies to every task regardless of perceived simplicity.

**Process:**
1. Explore project context (read relevant chapters, check cross-references)
2. Ask clarifying questions one at a time (prefer multiple choice)
3. Propose 2-3 approaches with trade-offs and a recommendation
4. Present the design in sections scaled to complexity
5. Get approval before proceeding
6. Write a design doc to `docs/superpowers/specs/YYYY-MM-DD-<topic>-design.md`
7. Self-review the spec for placeholders, contradictions, ambiguity, scope
8. Get user review of the written spec
9. Proceed to implementation

**Anti-pattern:** "This is too simple to need a design." Every project goes through brainstorming. Simple designs can be a few sentences, but the approval gate is non-negotiable.

**Scope check:** If a request describes multiple independent subsystems, decompose first. Each sub-project gets its own spec-plan-implementation cycle.

### Stop Slop (Prose Quality)

**Trigger:** Every piece of prose written or edited. All new text, all revisions, all expansions. Run this as a final pass before completing any writing task.

**8 Rules:**
1. Cut filler phrases. No throat-clearing openers, emphasis crutches, or adverbs.
2. Break formulaic structures. No binary contrasts, negative listings, dramatic fragmentation, rhetorical setups, false agency.
3. Use active voice. Every sentence needs a human subject doing something. No passive constructions. No inanimate objects performing human actions.
4. Be specific. No vague declaratives. Name the specific thing. No lazy extremes ("every," "always," "never").
5. Put the reader in the room. "You" beats "People." Specifics beat abstractions.
6. Vary rhythm. Mix sentence lengths. Two items beat three. No em dashes.
7. Trust readers. State facts directly. Skip softening, justification, hand-holding.
8. Cut quotables. If it sounds like a pull-quote, rewrite it.

**Quick checks before delivering prose:**
- Any adverbs? Kill them.
- Any passive voice? Find the actor, make them the subject.
- Inanimate thing doing a human verb? Name the person.
- Sentence starts with a Wh- word? Restructure it.
- "Here's what/this/that" throat-clearing? Cut to the point.
- "Not X, it's Y" contrasts? State Y directly.
- Three consecutive sentences match length? Break one.
- Paragraph ends with punchy one-liner? Vary it.
- Em-dash anywhere? Remove it.
- Vague declarative? Name the specific implication.

**Score 35+/50** on Directness, Rhythm, Trust, Authenticity, Density. Below 35: revise before delivering.

## Subagent and Orchestration Guidelines

### When to Use Subagents

Use the `Explore` subagent for:
- Searching the Setting Book for cross-references before making changes (character mentions, faction references, event citations across chapters)
- Gathering page numbers across multiple chapters to update the glossary or TOC
- Reading multiple files in parallel to build context (lore bible, phase1 design notes, style guide)
- Verifying consistency of a character's details across chapters

Specify thoroughness: `quick` (spot check), `medium` (relevant sections), `thorough` (full audit).

### Orchestration Patterns for This Repo

**Single chapter edit:**
1. Read the target chapter section
2. Read cross-referenced chapters (characters/events that appear elsewhere)
3. Make edits
4. Run Stop Slop pass on new prose
5. Update TOC if page count changed (count `\page` breaks)
6. Update glossary entries for any modified characters, locations, or terms

**New world or species entry:**
1. Brainstorm: read existing entries in Ch21/Ch22 for format and depth
2. Read the lore bible and factions-and-species supplement for source material
3. Propose structure and content outline, get approval
4. Write the entry
5. Run Stop Slop pass
6. Add glossary entries
7. Update TOC page numbers
8. Check if the world/species is mentioned in other chapters; add cross-references if needed

**Multi-chapter expansion (e.g., expanding all species entries):**
1. Brainstorm the full scope: which entries, what depth, what order
2. Decompose into independent sub-tasks (one per entry)
3. For each entry: read existing content → write expansion → Stop Slop pass → glossary update
4. After all entries: recount `\page` breaks, update full TOC, verify cross-references

**Cross-reference audit:**
- Use `Explore` subagent with `thorough` to find all mentions of a character/faction/term across the full Setting Book
- Compare details for consistency (ages, dates, titles, relationships)
- Fix discrepancies

## Prose Standards

All new prose must score 35+/50 on the Stop Slop rubric and pass the content quality standards below. Use the Star Wars Name Generator API (`https://vqcjvexnwxnwvaoeofka.supabase.co/functions/v1/generate-names`) for new named characters.

## Content Quality Standards

The expanded Alek (Ch8), Kaeranth (Ch9), Clan Ordo (Ch10), and Force Crisis POVs (Ch6) define the quality floor. These patterns are non-negotiable.

### Detail Density

No unnamed NPCs. No unquantified problems. No undated events.

| Content Type | Required Details |
|-------------|------------------|
| Characters | Age, homeworld, titles, named staff/rivals, specific Force abilities with degradation, physical description |
| Factions | Headcount, territory count, named leaders at multiple levels, specific ongoing operations with targets |
| Worlds | Named population centers, controlling authority, economic base with commodities, named local NPCs |
| Species | Named institutions, communication methods, specific cultural practices, named communities |

**Test:** Remove all proper nouns, numbers, and dates. If the section still reads coherently, it lacks detail.

### Actionable Campaign Material

Every paragraph must contain at least one actionable element: a decision pending, a conflict present, a secret to discover, a leverage point, or a resource to compete over.

| Section Type | Worldbuilding | Hooks | Reference |
|-------------|--------------|-------|----------|
| Origins / biography | 65% | 35% | Alek's childhood and career arc (Ch8) |
| Current operations | 40% | 60% | Kaeranth's supply raid plan, staff friction (Ch9) |
| Final operational paragraph | 30% | 70% | Clan Ordo's economic crisis, Fett rivalry (Ch10) |
| POV / perspective sections | 25% | 75% | Force crisis stakeholder perspectives (Ch6) |

**The Ch6 test:** "The Baran Do Sages issued their first incorrect forecast in 1 CE; three storms hit populated areas without warning" = mission seed, faction contact, timeline, stakes. "The Force is weakening and people are scared" = nothing. Every sentence must pass this test.

### Subsection Templates

**Characters:** Opening (title, age, location, power dynamic) → Origins (dates, mentors) → Force Capabilities (abilities, degradation) → Institutional Relationships (named subsections with friction) → Current Operations (routine, 2-3 quantified crises) → Player-facing closing

**Factions/Clans:** Opening (leader, headcount, territory) → Core ideology (stated as belief) → Quantified Problem 1 → Quantified Problem 2 → Named rival relationships → GM paragraph (entry points, navigation requirements)

**Worlds:** Opening (authority, strategic importance) → Geography (named districts/settlements) → Politics (who governs, who contests, quantified tension) → Economy/daily life (commodities, costs) → Force/crisis impact (specific changes) → Player hooks (3-4 scenarios)

**Species:** Opening (population status, homeworld, defining trait) → Social structure (governance, institutions) → Communication/culture (specific practices) → Current era pressures (wars, Force crisis, Concordat impact) → Diaspora/galactic role → PC implications (what a player from this species knows, believes, fears)

## Editing Workflow

When editing the Setting Book:

1. **Read first.** Read the relevant chapter section before making changes.
2. **Check cross-references.** Characters, events, and factions mentioned in one chapter appear in others. Changing a detail in Chapter 9 may require updates in Chapters 7, 8, 11, and the glossary.
3. **Update TOC.** After adding or removing content, recount `\page` breaks and update the Table of Contents if page numbers shifted.
4. **Update glossary.** After modifying character sections, update the glossary entry (descriptions and page numbers) in Appendix A.
5. **Stop Slop pass.** Scan new prose for em dashes, adverbs, passive voice, throat-clearing openers, binary contrasts, and filler phrases. Fix all violations before finishing.
6. **Hook test.** Every sentence should serve as a potential player or GM hook. If a sentence adds atmosphere without providing actionable campaign material, revise it until it does both.

## Fate Condensed System Reference

- 19 skills in a pyramid (1 Great +4, 2 Good +3, 3 Fair +2, 4 Average +1)
- Force-Sensitive Extra costs 1 stunt slot (2 free stunts instead of 3)
- Force techniques: Telekinesis, Precognition, Force Healing, Battle Meditation, Force Empathy, Force Concealment, Lightsaber Mastery, Force Persuasion
- Force Instability: GM has a standing compel against Force-related aspects
- Dead zone failure rates: ~1/3 normal space, ~2/3 near dead zones, fate point required in total dead zones
- Miraluka get Force Empathy free (Force perception is their baseline existence)
- Kolto: mild consequence clears immediately, moderate clears after 1 scene instead of 1 session
- Character creation driven by 6 narrative questions, not stats
